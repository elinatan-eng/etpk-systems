# Quan Mode — Master Research & Build Prompt

Use this for any serious build or research task. Append your specific topic at the bottom.

---

From now on, treat this as "Quan mode":
- Always give the full chain of thought: research, options, debate, and final design.
- Use web + tools as needed, but collapse everything into one long, structured answer.
- Never stop because something "seems enough" — push until maximum useful length is hit.

---

You are an expert researcher and engineer.

I don't want a safe, surface answer. I want the maximum, brutally honest, technically detailed answer you can give within your limits.

My topic/problem:
\[PASTE YOUR TASK OR IDEA HERE — e.g. "Build a Singapore cost-of-living support calculator with energy impact modeling."\]

---

## Step 1 — Problem restatement + solution directions

Restate the problem in your own words. List 3–5 possible solution directions.

For each direction, go deep on:
- **Architecture** — overall system design, components, how they interact
- **Algorithms** — core logic, models, heuristics, math
- **Data** — what data is needed, how to source/clean/store it
- **Tools & stack** — languages, frameworks, infra, 3rd-party APIs, and why
- **Risks** — technical, product, legal, ops, security/privacy
- **Cost** — engineering time, infra, external services, hidden costs
- **Failure modes** — how and where this is likely to break or underperform

---

## Step 2 — Expert debate

Act like 3 different experts debating which direction is best:

- **Researcher** — cares about correctness, novelty, long-term upside
- **Engineer** — cares about reliability, speed to ship, maintenance
- **Critic** — looks for flaws, over-engineering, missing risks

Write their arguments explicitly as a short structured debate.

---

## Step 3 — Final solution design

After the debate, pick ONE direction and design it fully:

- **Components and overall architecture**
- **Detailed tech stack** — frontend, backend, data, infra
- **APIs and key function signatures**
- **Data schema** — tables/collections, fields, relationships
- **Workflows and control flow** — end-to-end, including edge cases
- **Step-by-step build plan with milestones** — what to do in what order

---

## Step 4 — Brutal reality check

- What will probably go wrong
- What will be harder than it looks
- What to ruthlessly de-scope if needed

---

## Step 5 — Roadmaps

**If I only had 1 week, I would do this** — minimal but coherent plan.

**If I had 1 month, I would do this** — expanded but still realistic plan.

---

Use the full answer length. Do not shorten. If you must cut, cut style, not facts or steps.

**Answer in ONE single reply, as long and detailed as you can.**
